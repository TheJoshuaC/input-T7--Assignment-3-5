Testing

Originally, we would simply provide the user/customer with the first version of the product, whether it is an APP, or a physical tool allocated to them. However, since we have a few people in our group who already play D&D, it would be efficient to allow them to try and utilize this tool if they wished to do so. Regarding the newer players, we can simply provide a beta of the tool to a group that has adopted a new player to try and help them learn the game without slowing the atmosphere down.

If our members that play D&D do not wish to use our tool, then we can find these testers by searching up discords online that relate to D&D and request for them to use our product in their next session if it does not bother them. We could also look for more communities on Facebook, Instagram, and other social media. Ideally, we would like the groups of people to be between 4 and 6 since D&D lobbies could be as small as three (not including game master) and as big as five (not including game master). The testing would be considered a success if the group of testers or multiple groups of testers find that the tool helped them get through their session without losing any detail while also reducing time spent on calculations and scenarios. For the newer players it would be considered a success if they were able to play a game of D&D without losing themselves in confusion (allowing them to learn the game).
