Clouds, Services and Servers (Technology 1) - By Amani Zafirah Mohammad Anwar


Q1:

Clouds, services, and servers are three interconnected technologies that have revolutionised the way businesses and individuals’ access and use computing resources. Clouds are essentially large networks of servers that are connected to the internet and used to store, process, and manage data remotely. This technology allows users to access and use these resources on demand, without the need to invest in and maintain their own hardware. There are three main types of clouds: public, private, and hybrid (Azure, 2023). Public clouds are owned and operated by third-party companies and are available to the public on a pay-per-use basis. Private clouds, on the other hand, are owned and operated by a single organisation and are used to store and process sensitive or proprietary data. Hybrid clouds are a combination of public and private clouds, allowing organisations to take advantage of the benefits of both. Cloud services, also known as cloud-based services, are applications or services that are delivered over the internet from a cloud computing provider (Enslin, 2021). These services can be used by individuals or businesses to access a wide range of computing resources, including storage, networking, software, analytics, and so on. Some examples of cloud services include email, document management, customer relationship management, and project management. On the other hand, servers are physical or virtual machines that are used to store, process, and manage data and resources. They are an integral part of any cloud or cloud-based service, as they provide the computing power and storage capacity needed to support these technologies. The state of the art of this new technology is constantly evolving, with new advancements being made in areas such as cloud security, data management, and artificial intelligence. One of the most significant developments in recent years has been the emergence of edge computing, which involves processing data and running applications at the edge of the network, closer to the user or device. This allows for faster data processing and reduced latency, as data does not need to be transmitted to and from a central location. Currently, clouds, services, and servers are used in a wide range of applications, including data storage and backup, data analytics, web hosting, and software development. They are also used by organisations to scale up or down their computing resources as needed, allowing them to be more agile and responsive to changing business needs. In the next three years, it is likely that we will see further advancements in the use of artificial intelligence and machine learning in the cloud (Kumar et al. 2019:894). This will enable organisations to analyse and process large amounts of data more efficiently and accurately, leading to improved decision making and operational efficiency. We may also see the emergence of more specialized clouds, such as those focused on healthcare or finance, as well as increased adoption of hybrid clouds as organizations seek to balance the benefits of public and private clouds. The technological developments that have made these advancements possible include advances in networking and data storage technologies, as well as the proliferation of powerful and affordable computing devices. The widespread adoption of the internet and cloud technologies has also played a key role in the growth and development of these technologies. Overall, clouds, services, and servers have had a significant impact on the way businesses and individuals’ access and use computing resources. As these technologies continue to evolve and mature, we can expect to see even more transformative applications and capabilities in the coming years.

Natural Language Processing (Technology 2) - By Joshua Christiansen

Computer science is being used to solve problems that would take an exceptionally longer amount of time to process by a human. One of these problems computer science is trying to solve is natural language processing. Natural language processing (NLP from here onwards) is the study and application of making computers understand natural human language. The types of information that are being deciphered through NLP systems include computer typed writing, such as word documents and blog posts, to speech recognition software like Apple’s Siri or Google’s Voice Assistant. The goal of NLP is to be able to understand and communicate through human language as fluently as if we were speaking with another native speaker of the language being understood. This is an incredibly complicated process, the amount of sentence structures (at the very least in English) we can create are in a practical sense limitless. When it comes to understanding written or spoken words, the context can change meaning in a sentence. Two different situations where a person would say the exact same sentence could convey an entirely different meaning. To this day we are still unable to make things like chatbots that can mimic a real human, both with its ability to understand what is being said to it and then communicating back with human like intelligence.

When we speak or write, we can communicate through a system in which we can derive meaning. Like a list of groceries, we can look at the list of different items and understand they are things we need to purchase due to them being under the heading of groceries. However, if I were to type those individual items into a computer with the header “Grocery list” it would not grab any meaning from the words typed. It would rather take the information at face value and turn it into a user input value that is equal to itself. The first job of an NLP is to turn the user input, regardless of if it is through text or speech, into something the computer can understand. It can organize the data given into categories that give meaning to the computer, and more importantly, be used by the computer to understand what is being asked of it by the user. This is no easy feat even for humans.

I personally have a huge interest in sociology and world cultures. One of the most amazing and fascinating things to me is that no matter which language we choose, it cannot be translated perfectly to any other. Language itself is not a universal system of equal values like mathematics. Imagine you are in an arts class and the teacher instructs every person to create something that can teach you how to make a cake, but you cannot use numbers or letters. You can produce an idea in your head and though it is not perfect, it translates to you how you would make a cake. Maybe a few will not paint but create sculptures that are a mixture of all “steps” put together in some form. It is possible to say that some of them may create artwork that does not tell you how to make a cake but gives you the feeling of what baking feels like.

Natural language is a lot like the art pieces in that room, everyone has a unique way of saying what could be considered the same piece of information. But they really are not the same and depending on which student you learned to communicate with, would make every other students language seem like the most bizarre thing on the planet due to the number of ways we can understand the world around us. We think the way we communicate, and that is only one perspective in an infinite world of ways to think. Now go and show your art piece to a computer and see if it can understand even 10% of the instructions you gave it.

NLPs now use a variety of semantic analysis processes to derive meaning and interpretations. This is a fun way of saying that for the computer to understand what is given, we are using and creating different linguistic equations that can translate what is being said. However, as linguistics is not a science, as of today it is near impossible to create a singular algorithm that can translate language accurately between humans and computers. It is unlikely that we will have a system that can do this perfectly within the next 10 years. We are using deep learning neural networks to make a mathematical communication model that will eventually lead to having enough data to mimic fluid comprehension of human linguistics.

This field is enormous, both in the scope of what it is doing and what it hopes to achieve. The goal is to make computers understand humans, and humans to understand computers. This can be used for things as simple as telling your phone to organize a bunch of items to be delivered to your house through speech, all the way to having a computer teach people new skills and topics through active communication. We can guess that anything that requires you to communicate with another human could be potentially replaced with a computer using NLP software. That is a crazy thought, that we could speak and be spoken to by computers that understand us as much as any other person would. A program could be taking your order from a business, giving you mental health therapy, providing comfort and companionship to people who are not getting enough interactivity with others, teach classes through open discussion and resolve disputes between others. Cyber Security (Technology 3) - By Khaled Elmasri


Cyber security is the sole intent of protecting systems, networks, programs or anything similar and has been around since 1972 which started with a project related to ARPANET which was a precursor to the internet. The state of the art for cyber security for this time currently would be the certification ISO 27001. ISO/IEC 27000 series as the name implies is a part of a set of standards developed to help with information security. The full name of the ISO 27001 is “ISO/IEC 27001 – Information security, cyber security and privacy protection – information security management systems – requirements”. This certification is a standard that utilizes an Information and Security management System while also allowing businesses of any size to manage and store their information safely in a cost effective and in a systematic way. ISO 27001 was published by the joint technical committee of “International Organization for Standardization (ISO)” and “International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC)”. Both organisations develop, manage and update similar international standards

This standard allows businesses and companies with the necessary knowledge to protect their information; however there are businesses that get certified against ISO 27001; the business may do it for the reputation of best practice or to reassure customers and clients. This means that customers and anyone else involved can trust the business to protect any of the data confided in them because they follow set standards. These standards must be kept and assessed regularly if ISO is to continue supplying the business with the certification however being certified by ISO 27001 means that a person has attended and completed a course setup by ISO and IEC that proves their skill in regard to changing and/or fixing an Information Security Management System for potential customers and or employers. One of the many upsides of ISO 27001 is that it “is easily recognised all around the world, increasing business opportunities for organizations and professionals” (Kosutic, Dejan (No date))[1]. Every standard related to ISO and IEC have principles that outline what is the basic goal of said standard.

Iso's principles are:

- Confidentiality meaning only the people related to the data may access the information.

- Integrity is that only authorized people may change the information.

- Availability to authorized personnel whenever needed.

The basic framework of ISO 27001 is reliant on the processes of managing risks. This implies that the people under ISO 27001 will assess possible risks and incidents that could happen and attempt to find a solution that needs to be implemented to help prevent any of those proceeding risks or incidents from occurring. ISO 27001 has implemented “controls”, which are referred to as safeguards. These safeguards are “practices to be implemented to reduce risks to acceptable levels” (ISO/IEC 27001 and related standards – information security management, 2022) [3], these safeguards are (but not limited to) technological, organizational, physical and human-related.

With ISO 27001 businesses in varying size can secure their information in all forms and being cost effective. Meaning that paper, cloud and digital based data is safe and secure. This also increases the businesses resilience to cyber-attacks. In utilizing the ISO 27001 standard the business is provided a system that manages all of its information all in one place while also ensuring a definitive way to defend it from technological-based attacks or other threats which also directly translates into having the ability to defend itself from threats that are continually evolving in cyber security. In the near future it is very possible that there will be less technologically based attacks as the ISO and IEC unity will continue to improve upon its standards which will improve the safety and procedures conducted in the next five years. It would also be correct to assume that the tests to become certified or certified against will improve. There isn’t really any technological advancement that impacts these changes; it’s more or less the people behind ISO and IEC that make these changes possible as it is a standard that people study and follow. Thus making businesses and individuals more secure with their data.

As a result of the increase in security of the business it will make for a safer and more successful change for businesses that require the user to confide their data in a business such as websites, banks, companies etc. For e.g. a business or individual that has procured a certification for or against will have a safer and reliable customer base as most customers will have known about the ISO 27001 standards allowing for a more profitable business in the long run. It will also be true for the opposite, being that people who do not have these certifications will not be as successful since the customers will have a harder time trusting the business in protecting their data. This will also lower the chances in which a business is attacked by hackers or outsider threats because of a business’s decision to either get their employees tested or get certified against by ISO and IEC as it would give the employees vital knowledge which would increase the overall resilience to cyber attacks due to taking the test. On the other side of the spectrum, the smaller businesses that can’t afford it and don’t have the verification would experience a harder time surviving attacks since most of the cyber attacks would funnel onto them as their defence is weaker when compared to those who do have the certification. This would affect the cyber security professionals that test the resilience of businesses as most employees or the business will have knowledge and the necessary controls (safeguards) to check for their own systems for risks and issues. The cyber security professionals would have received fewer commissions to be able to do their job, which would eventually make their jobs redundant as a result of the certification.
